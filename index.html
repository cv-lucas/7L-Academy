The 7 Liberal Arts

  1. Grammar

    Like everything metaphysical the harmony between thought and reality is to be found in the grammar of the language.

    Ludwig Wittgenstein

  2. Logic

    Logic is the most useful tool of all the arts. Without it no science can be fully known.

    William of Ockham

  3. Rhetoric

    Rhetoric is the art of ruling the minds of men.

    Plato

  4. Arithmetic

    Mathematics is the queen of science, and arithmetic the queen of mathematics.

    Gauss

  5. Geometry

    Geometry is one and eternal shining in the mind of God.

    Johannes Kepler

  6. Music

    Music is an agreeable harmony for the honor of God and the permissible delights of the soul.

    J. S. Bach

  7. Astronomy

    Of all things visible, the highest is the heaven of the fixed stars.

    Copernicus

"Instruction in the liberal arts, if only it is moderate and concise, produces devotees more alert and steadfast and better equipped for embracing truth."
St. Augustin
